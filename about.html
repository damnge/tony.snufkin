ABOUT ME

I’m a visual artist and photographer based in Iceland, with a background in street photography, photojournalism, and visual storytelling. My work is shaped by a curiosity about culture and everyday life—interests that have led me to document people and places across continents.

I was first drawn to photography through the raw, unscripted rhythm of the street. Over the years, I’ve captured fleeting moments in cities like Tehran, Bangkok, Dubai, São Paulo, Sydney, and Melbourne—observing how light, shadow, movement, and human presence form quiet, often overlooked scenes. I’m especially interested in the tension between stillness and chaos, anonymity and character. My approach is intuitive and grounded in a desire to preserve fragments of time.

Lately, I’ve expanded into film photography and abstract visual forms, exploring slower, more contemplative methods. These practices complement my documentary eye and create space for more personal, atmospheric narratives.

If you’re interested in working together or would like to inquire about prints, feel free to get in touch.
